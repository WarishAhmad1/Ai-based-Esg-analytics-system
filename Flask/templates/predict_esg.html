How Overall ESG Score is Calculated

The Overall ESG Score is calculated using a weighted scoring methodology based on the three major sustainability pillars:

  • Environmental (E)
  • Social (S)
  • Governance (G)

Instead of using a simple average, the system applies professional ESG weights commonly inspired by industry ESG frameworks.

ESG Formula

Overall ESG = (0.4 × Environmental) + (0.3 × Social) + (0.3 × Governance)

Why Different Weights are Used?

Environmental sustainability is given higher importance because modern industries face significant climate-related and environmental risks.

Factors such as:

  • Carbon Emissions
  • Energy Consumption
  • Water Usage
  • Pollution Impact
  • Environmental Regulations

directly affect long-term business sustainability, investor trust, regulatory compliance, and corporate reputation.

Therefore, the Environmental pillar receives 40% weight, while Social and Governance factors receive 30% weight each.

What the ESG Pillars Represent

  • Environmental (40%)
    Measures environmental impact such as emissions, energy efficiency, sustainability practices, and resource consumption.

  • Social (30%)
    Measures employee welfare, diversity, customer relations, labor practices, and social responsibility.

  • Governance (30%)
    Measures company ethics, board structure, transparency, compliance, and risk management practices.

Industry-Level ESG Methodology

This weighted ESG scoring approach is inspired by professional sustainability assessment methodologies used in modern ESG analytics and financial risk evaluation.

The system combines Machine Learning predictions with weighted ESG evaluation to generate a more realistic and industry-oriented sustainability score for organizations.
